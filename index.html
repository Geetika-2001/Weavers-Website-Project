MEET THE WEAVERS

Carefully handcrafted with love

The weaver continuously presses a pedal to move the sutras (heald shafts) up and down , while the shuttle is manually inserted.When a row of weft is finished, the weaver strengthens it with the use of a sattar. The weavers have to pedal fast and pull the sattar quickly. When numerous colours are utilised, the weaver examines the pattern on the graph to determine when the thread colour has to be changed.The durry is concurrently rolled on a beam. When the length of the desired durry is received, it is removed from the loom and sent for finishing.

His family comprises of his wife and two children and both of them work in different fields and doesn’t practice the craft as it has no income according to their father. Karim has said that he does weaving as a laborious task, and has no other side business for income.

Pitta Ramulu

Age -52

Pitta Ramulu started his weaving journey at the age of 12, after his sixth standard. He procured the techniques from his parents as it was a traditional practice where his father P. Rajeeru, did Khadi weaving and his mother practiced spinning and winding. The family lineage broke when his kids did not continue the practice of weaving even after the attaining the knowledge and techniques of weaving
